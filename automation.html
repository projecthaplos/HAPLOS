The Automation Behind HAPLOS

A controlled mechanical workflow transforms pressed coconut fiber pads into guava-infused gauze through precise extract dosing, actuator-guided transfer, and standardized starch coating for consistent structural quality.

1. Extract Dispensing

A calibrated pump dispenses a fixed volume of guava leaf extract into the infusion tray. This ensures that each production cycle begins with a controlled extract quantity, reducing variability in absorption.

2. Automated Pad Placement

A pre-cut and pressed coconut fiber pad is pushed from the storage compartment onto the infusion tray using a linear electric actuator. The actuator ensures consistent placement and stable contact with the extract.

3. Infusion and Tray Rotation

The coconut fiber pad absorbs the guava extract through direct tray contact. After absorption, the tray rotates mechanically, allowing the infused pad to transfer into the next stage using gravity-assisted movement.

4. Starch Coating and Distribution

A second linear electric actuator pushes the infused pad through the coating section. A measured starch mixture composed of xanthan gum, glycerin, and cassava starch drips from above, while a silicone brush spreads the coating evenly across the pad surface.

5. Pad Release and Air Drying

The finished pad exits the system and is collected for natural air drying. This passive drying method preserves structural integrity and prevents damage to the infused material.
