The Process Behind HAPLOS Pads

Each gauze pad is produced by combining three carefully developed components: a bioactive guava extract, mechanically prepared coconut fibers, and a cassava-starch binder. Below is a clear, step-by-step view of each track and how they merge into the final product.

Guava Extract

Collection

Fresh guava leaves are carefully harvested and sorted to remove any damaged material.

Drying

Leaves are dried under controlled conditions to preserve phenolics and other phytochemicals.

Ethanol Extraction

Dried leaves are submerged in 95% ethanol to extract active compounds (Psidium guajava L.).

Filtration & Concentration

Solution is filtered and concentrated to yield a consistent guava extract for infusion.

Coconut Fibers

Collection & Rinse

Husk fibers are collected, rinsed in clean water to remove soil and impurities.

Softening

Fibers are boiled in a mild sodium bicarbonate solution to soften and loosen the material.

Sterilization

Hydrogen peroxide or validated sterilization method applied to reduce bioburden.

Standardization

Fibers are dried and cut into standardized sheets and sizes for assembly.

Cassava Binder

Formulation

Cassava starch, xanthan gum, glycerin and distilled water are measured precisely.

Mixing

Starch slurry is mixed with cold distilled water, then combined with xanthan/glycerin.

Heating

The binder is heated to ~65°C while stirring to achieve the correct viscosity.

Cooling & Stabilizing

The binder is cooled and kept at controlled conditions ready for sealing pads.

Coconut-fiber gauze sheets are infused with the standardized guava extract and sealed with the cassava starch binder — producing the final Guava-Infused Coconut Fiber Gauze Pads. All steps are designed for repeatability and to maintain bioactivity and structural integrity.
